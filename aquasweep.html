The Solution

Engineered to collect,
not just observe.

01

AI-Guided Navigation

AquaSweep's onboard computer system uses satellite imagery and real-time sensors to map debris concentrations and autonomously navigate to the highest-density zones. No human operator required.

02

Surface Veil Collection

A wide-mouth passive intake system — the "veil" — spans up to 8 meters at the bow. As the vessel moves, water flows through fine mesh screens that trap debris from macro-plastic down to 0.3mm particles.

03

Onboard Sorting & Compression

Collected material is conveyed into an automated sorting chamber where AI vision identifies material types. Plastics are compacted into dense bales. Organic matter is separated for safe return to the water.

04

Zero-Emission Propulsion

Solar panels across the deck and a hydrogen fuel cell backup system power the vessel entirely without combustion emissions. AquaSweep cleans the ocean without adding to the pollution chain.

05

Continuous Deployment

Fleet-managed via cloud dashboard. Vessels operate in coordinated sweeps, uploading real-time data on waste collected, GPS position, and ocean health metrics to our global monitoring platform.

06

Port Offloading & Recycling

When storage reaches capacity, AquaSweep autonomously returns to designated port facilities where compacted material is offloaded and transferred directly to certified plastic recycling or upcycling partners.

Core Technology

Four systems.
One mission.

System 01 — Veil Array

The Surface Veil

A patented passive-flow collection net system engineered from ultra-fine HDPE mesh stretched across a carbon fiber boom. The veil is deployed at the bow, angled into the current. As water passes through, debris is captured and conveyed aft via a low-energy belt system. Marine organisms are protected by biocompatible mesh pores and current-avoidance sensors.

System 02 — NeuralSweep AI

Intelligent Navigation

NeuralSweep is our proprietary onboard AI that fuses satellite ocean-surface data, lidar point clouds, and hull-mounted radar to build live debris density maps. The system plans optimal collection routes, dynamically avoids marine wildlife, coordinates with other fleet vessels to prevent redundant sweeping, and uploads hourly telemetry to shore operations.

System 03 — SortCore

Onboard Sorting Engine

Collected material drops onto a conveyor beneath a computer-vision scanner trained to identify over 80 material types: PET bottles, HDPE packaging, fishing line, foam, metal, and more. Organic matter (seaweed, fish, shells) is identified and safely returned overboard via a bypass chute. Plastics are segregated by polymer type and compressed into 40kg bales.

System 04 — ZeroTrace Drive

Emissions-Free Propulsion

AquaSweep's propulsion system pairs a 24 kWp flexible solar array laminated to the deck with a 40 kWh lithium-iron-phosphate battery bank and a hydrogen fuel cell as a deep reserve. Dual azimuth thrusters provide precise maneuvering. The vessel generates more energy than it consumes on average, leaving a net positive energy balance over a full deployment cycle.
