The Vessel

Built and tested
in the field.

Hull & Structure

The MK-I catamaran hull features a shallow-draft aluminium frame spanning 1.5m, purpose-built for inland waterways. The wide beam ensures stability in canal and river conditions while the low profile allows passage under infrastructure.

Collection System

A passive-intake veil system spans 0.5m at the bow, funnelling floating debris into the onboard collection bay. As the vessel advances, surface material is guided through fine mesh screens — capturing waste from macro-plastic down to small particles without harming aquatic life.

Navigation & AI

AquaSweep's onboard system fuses lidar point clouds and hull-mounted sensors to build real-time debris density maps. The semi-autonomous navigation plans optimal collection routes while uploading live telemetry — speed, heading, battery, and storage — to the shore operations dashboard.

Field Deployment

AquaSweep is tested in real urban waterway conditions at the Peel Basin of the Lachine Canal, Montréal — developed by Concordia University engineering students in association with Parks Canada. The April 27th public demonstration is the culmination of months of design, build, and iteration.

Core Technology

Four systems.
One mission.

System 01 — ZeroTrace Drive

Emissions-Free Propulsion

AquaSweep’s propulsion system pairs a high-torque dual BLDC motor configuration with a robust 1.25 kWh energy reserve. Powered by two 50Ah deep-cycle batteries, the system delivers consistent, reliable thrust for sustained operations. The dual-motor architecture ensures precise maneuvering and optimal energy efficiency, providing more than enough power to navigate challenging aquatic environments with a minimal environmental footprint.

System 02 — Collector Array

The Collection Mechanism

AquaSweep's conveyor belt is custom-designed and 3D-printed from lightweight, waterproof PLA composite, allowing rapid prototyping and field-replaceable modular sections. The belt runs on a variable-speed motor, adjustable in real time based on debris density — slowing for heavy loads and accelerating in sparse conditions to maximize coverage. Debris scooped at the bow is carried continuously aft to the onboard storage bay with no manual intervention required throughout the deployment cycle.

System 03 — NeuralSweep AI

Intelligent Navigation

NeuralSweep is AquaSweep's onboard computer vision system, powered by a fine-tuned YOLO model trained on waterway debris datasets. Camera mounted at the bow feeds a live video stream into the inference pipeline, enabling the vessel to detect and classify floating waste in real time. The system builds dynamic debris density maps , plan optimal collection routes, and upload continuous telemetry to shore operations for remote monitoring.

System 04 — BioGuard AI

Marine Life Preservation

BioGuard is a secondary computer vision model running in parallel with NeuralSweep, dedicated entirely to wildlife protection. A camera trained on aquatic fauna continuously scans the conveyor belt during operation. If any live animal — fish, amphibian, or waterfowl — is detected on the belt, BioGuard immediately halts the conveyor and triggers an alert, preventing harm before the animal is returned safely to the water. Collection only resumes once the belt is confirmed clear.
